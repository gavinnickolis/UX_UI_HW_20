Chef's Kiss Case Study

This site will go over all that was done in the case study for the prototyped app that is Chef's Kiss. Chef’s Kiss is a mobile application that allows you to share your recipes to your friends! Not only that, but you can comment, like, favorite recipes and much more! Have a great recipe? The more liked your recipe, the higher your listing will rise. Search by cooking time, ingredients, region, or find a new flavor just exploring the app!

User Research

With the modern busy lifestyle, meal ideas should be as fast and easy as a simple search. Chef's Kiss aims to bring people of all culinary skill sets together to share the joy of home cooking.
People who have readily available ideas for meals are better prepared to cook than those who don't have any ideas on what to make
We began by interviewing 3 people in order to understand how many people are cooking their own food versus eating out, and why they eat that way. In our interviewees there was a high frequency of individuals who wanted to cook more but ran into hurdles when trying to get it done

Due to strenuous life schedules, our consumers need a quick and easy app to generate meal ideas and find recipes.
Current platforms with recipe ideas either take too long or are too complicated to complete. We have observed that a lot of people do not have a place to share their recipe ideas nor do they have the time to find new, simple ones. This causes them to create the same meals regularly. How might we create a new mobile application so that our users have access to a variety of different, simple meal options they’re able to make?

The competitors for the Chef’s Kiss app were very valuable to look through when going forward with designing the UI to our app. One example being that both direct competitors, Tasty and Kitchen Stories utilized pictures heavily in their apps. This allowed us to view what already successful apps were already using that we could implement into our app. While the indirect competitors allowed us to view what our potential users would see in alternative options.

Usability Testing

View our interactive Prototype!
User Testing Goal Testing Synopsis User Flow Iteration Final Thoughts
Is Chef’s Kiss intuitive enough to where our customers can find recipes of all levels of difficulty and variety?
During testing we realized that our prototype was overly complicated. In other words, there were too many ways to complete each task. This caused some user confusion and a need for explanation of certain features. Due to these findings, we eliminated certain features and iterated our user flow.
When working as a team on this project the process went a lot quicker than it did when we were working individually. In other words, it streamlined the brainstorming process. With Chef’s Kiss, the app was simple enough that we believe could help a variety of people. If we had more time, something we would like to propose for a future iteration is a search screen. Another feature we did not get to develop was how a user could friend people and how that could influence how they find recipes.
